# Core Transition Selection

**Chosen transition:** C → D (Rover Safety → Pneumatic-tire Safety, 1888)

**Justification:**
This transition shows the clearest case where subjective pressure meaningfully improves explanatory power. The objective improvement (pneumatic comfort) is well-documented via Dunlop's 1888 invention and race results. However, the hybrid interpretation captures why this technical change triggered a mass adoption wave rather than a niche upgrade: comfort lowered psychological barriers, making cycling socially acceptable for women and reducing public health anxieties (evidenced by the “bicycle face” debate in 1895 newspapers and scholarly analysis of 1890s women's advertising). The pressure annotation connects a physical change (tire) to demographic expansion and cultural discourse, demonstrating TPR's value beyond pure engineering history.

**Why other transitions were weaker:**
- **A → B (lever → chain):** Primarily a mechanical efficiency upgrade; subjective factors minimal. Hybrid adds little.
- **B → C (Bicyclette → Rover):** Objective stability gains dominate; “safety” marketing present but not decisive. Hybrid improvement modest.

C → D is the strongest candidate because subjective evidence is both available and consequential.
