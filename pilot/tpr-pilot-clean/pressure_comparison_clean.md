# Pressure Annotation Comparison: Objective-Only vs Hybrid

To evaluate the value of subjective-pressure modeling, we compare two explanatory frames for each transition.

## A → B: Lever → Chain

**Objective-only:** Chain drive improved mechanical efficiency and power transfer over levers; rear-wheel configuration avoided front-spoke entanglement. A straightforward technical improvement.

**Hybrid:** Adds that the “safety” brand, established with the lever machine, carried into the chain-driven design, reinforcing its non-dangerous image. However, evidence for subjective influence here is thin; the change is primarily technical.

**Comparison:** Negligible difference. Subjective pressure not consequential.

## B → C: Bicyclette → Rover

**Objective-only:** Equal wheel sizing and diamond frame improved stability, simplified manufacturing, and reduced cost. These objective advantages explain the Rover's commercial success.

**Hybrid:** Adds that the Rover was more than objectively better—it was *marketed* as “safe” at a moment when public wariness of high-wheelers was high. Contemporary advertisements (1885) explicitly use “safety” language. This likely accelerated adoption beyond what pure technical merits would have produced.

**Comparison:** Hybrid provides a materially better explanation. Objective factors created a viable product; subjective factors helped it dominate the market.

## C → D: Solid Tires → Pneumatic

**Objective-only:** Pneumatic tires provided a smoother, faster ride via better shock absorption and lower rolling resistance. This extended practical cycling range and endurance.

**Hybrid:** Adds that the comfort revolution changed cycling's social meaning. With reduced vibration and fatigue, cycling became acceptable to women (a major new user group) and less associated with health hazards (the “bicycle face” controversy). The technology altered not just the ride but the rider demographics and cultural perceptions.

**Comparison:** Hybrid substantially improves explanation. Objective factors explain the physical upgrade; subjective factors explain the dramatic 1890s cycling boom and democratization of the activity.

## Outcome

- A→B: hybrid ≈ objective-only
- B→C: hybrid moderately stronger
- C→D: hybrid clearly stronger

**Conclusion:** For this artifact, subjective pressure annotation meaningfully enhances explanatory power in at least one key transition (C→D). This supports a disciplined Outcome B (hybrid slightly better overall) rather than maximalist claims.
