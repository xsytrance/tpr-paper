## Core Finding

Hybrid annotation (objective + subjective pressures) adds meaningful explanatory power in one key transition (C→D: pneumatic tires), where comfort improvements enabled mass adoption and changed the social meaning of cycling. Earlier transitions remain objectively dominated. This supports a cautious but positive conclusion: TPR's subjective pressure layer can materially improve interpretation, though its value varies by artifact phase.

## Quick Reference

**Chosen transition:** C → D (Rover Safety → Pneumatic-tire Safety, 1888)  
**Why:** Subjective pressures are clearly evidenced (advertising, press commentary, cultural analysis) and materially improve explanation of the 1890s cycling boom.  
**Outcome:** Outcome B — hybrid slightly better overall; not always decisive, but demonstrably useful.
