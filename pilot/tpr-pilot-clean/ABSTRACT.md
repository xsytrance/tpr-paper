# Pilot Abstract

**Question:** Can a small material artifact lineage be represented as a versioned, provenance-aware repository with disciplined subjective-pressure annotation?

**Approach:** Reconstructed a 4-commit lineage of the safety bicycle (1876–1892) from patents, museum objects, period ads, newspapers, and modern scholarship. Each commit records physical specs, objective pressures (safety, cost, performance), subjective pressures (perceived safety, social acceptability), causal claims with evidence tags, and confidence.

**Core result:** The pneumatic-tire transition (C→D, 1888) shows that subjective pressures materially improve explanation: comfort improvements enabled women's cycling and reduced health anxieties—factors absent from an objective-only account. Earlier transitions (lever→chain) are primarily technical; hybrid annotation adds little.

**Conclusion:** The TPR framework is feasible at small scale and can capture culturally relevant pressures without speculation. The pilot meets all success criteria (≥3 commits, ≥12 sources, clear source-backed subjective example). Outcome B: hybrid annotation is modestly but demonstrably valuable.
